## 🧠 Knowledge Library

### Behavioural Science

- [[02-Knowledge Library/Behavioral Science Frameworks/EAST Framework - BIT\|EAST Framework - BIT]] — Make it Easy, Attractive, Social, Timely. The go-to practitioner checklist for designing nudges and interventions.
- [[02-Knowledge Library/Behavioral Science Frameworks/COM-B Model - Michie et al\|COM-B Model - Michie et al]] — Behaviour = Capability + Opportunity + Motivation. Use this to diagnose WHY a behaviour isn't happening.
- [[02-Knowledge Library/Behavioral Science Frameworks/Behaviour Change Wheel - Michie et al\|Behaviour Change Wheel - Michie et al]] — The full framework built on COM-B: 9 intervention functions × 7 policy categories. Use this when you need to systematically choose what TYPE of intervention to design.
- [[02-Knowledge Library/Behavioral Science Frameworks/Nudge Frameworks\|Nudge Frameworks]] — Overview of nudge theory, choice architecture, and when nudges are (and aren't) appropriate.
- [[Cognitive Biases Cheat Sheet\|Cognitive Biases Cheat Sheet]] — Quick reference for the biases and heuristics we encounter most in our work.
- [[Social Norms\|Social Norms]] — How what others do and think influences behaviour. Includes when social norms backfire.
- [[Loss Aversion\|Loss Aversion]] — We feel losses roughly twice as much as equivalent gains. One of the most reliable findings in behavioural science.
- [[Framing Effects\|Framing Effects]] — The same information presented differently leads to different decisions.
- [[Friction Costs and Sludge\|Friction Costs and Sludge]] — Small barriers that stop people from acting, and how to audit for them.

### Design Thinking

- [[HCD Principles\|HCD Principles]] — Human-centred design basics: empathise, define, ideate, prototype, test.
- [[Double Diamond Explained\|Double Diamond Explained]] — The classic design process: Discover → Define → Develop → Deliver.
- [[Understand-Design-Test Framework\|Understand-Design-Test Framework]] — The 5D process used in our MOM BI training: Define, Discover, Diagnose, Design, Test.

### Systems Thinking

- [[Causal Loop Diagrams\|Causal Loop Diagrams]] — How to map feedback loops and unintended consequences in complex systems.
- [[Systems Mapping Guide\|Systems Mapping Guide]] — Step-by-step guide to creating system maps for problem scoping.

### Research Methods

- [[Qualitative Methods\|Qualitative Methods]] — Interviews, focus groups, observation, ethnography, diary studies.
- [[Quantitative Methods\|Quantitative Methods]] — Surveys, experiments, statistical analysis.
- [[Survey Design\|Survey Design]] — How to design surveys that actually give you useful data.
- [[Interview Guides\|Interview Guides]] — Tips for running in-depth interviews and focus group discussions.

### Evaluation Methods

- [[RCT Basics\|RCT Basics]] — Randomised controlled trials: the gold standard and when/how to use them.
- [[Pre-Post Analysis\|Pre-Post Analysis]] — Measuring before and after an intervention, and why you need a control group.
- [[Quasi-Experimental Methods\|Quasi-Experimental Methods]] — Practical alternatives when RCTs aren't possible (difference-in-differences, propensity score matching, etc.).
- [[Process Evaluation\|Process Evaluation]] — Understanding HOW and WHY an intervention worked (or didn't), not just WHETHER it did.

### Training Materials

- [[02-Knowledge Library/Training Materials/MOM Introductory BI Programme - Day 1 (Understand)\|MOM Introductory BI Programme - Day 1 (Understand)]] — Covers System 1 & 2, biases, the Understand phase (Define → Discover → Diagnose), user research methods. 157 slides.
- [[02-Knowledge Library/Training Materials/MOM Introductory BI Programme - Day 2 (Design and Test)\|MOM Introductory BI Programme - Day 2 (Design and Test)]] — Covers BI solutioning, ideation, prototyping, qualitative evaluation, and quantitative evaluation (RCTs, quasi-experiments). 125 slides.
