# 🧠 Welcome to Our Team's Second Brain

**What is this place?**

Think of this vault as our team's shared memory. It's where we keep everything we know, everything we've learned, and everything we're working on — all in one place that anyone on the team can search, browse, and add to.

Before this, our knowledge was scattered everywhere — in people's heads, buried in email chains, lost in random folders, or locked inside slide decks nobody opens twice. This vault changes that. When someone learns something useful, it goes here. When someone finishes a project, the lessons go here. When someone joins the team, they start here.

**The goal is simple:** no one on this team should ever have to figure out something that someone else already figured out.

---

## 🗂️ How This Vault Is Organised

We have five main areas. Think of them like rooms in a building.

### 📁 00 - Home Base (You are here!)

This is the front door. It has this welcome note, our team agreements, a glossary of terms we use, and a **Map of Content** — which is basically a homepage with links to the most important notes in the vault. If you're ever lost, come back here.

### 📁 01 - Projects

This is where the actual work lives. Every project gets its own folder, and inside each project folder you'll find a consistent structure that follows our workflow:

1. **Scoping & Diagnostics** — What's the problem? Who's affected? What do we already know?
2. **Research & Discovery** — What did we go out and learn? Interviews, surveys, observations, desk research.
3. **Insights & Synthesis** — What does it all mean? Patterns, behavioural insights, system maps.
4. **Design & Prototyping** — What solutions are we proposing? Interventions, nudges, service changes.
5. **Testing & Trials** — How are we testing it? Trial protocols, methodology, data collection.
6. **Evaluation & Results** — Did it work? What happened? What do the numbers say?

Every project also has a **Project Brief** (the "what and why") and a **Retrospective** (the "what did we learn for next time").

**Rule of thumb:** If it belongs to a specific project, it goes in that project's folder.

### 📁 02 - Knowledge Library

This is our team's textbook — except it's one that keeps growing. It holds knowledge that is **not tied to any single project** but is useful across all our work.

You'll find sections on:

- **Behavioural Science** — Biases, heuristics, nudge frameworks, key theories
- **Design Thinking** — Human-centred design, the double diamond, understand-design-test
- **Systems Thinking** — Causal loops, system maps, complexity
- **Research Methods** — Qualitative, quantitative, surveys, interviews
- **Evaluation Methods** — RCTs, pre-post analysis, process evaluations

**Rule of thumb:** If a future team member would find it useful even if they never work on the project you learned it from, it belongs in the Knowledge Library.

### 📁 03 - Templates & Playbooks

Templates are blank forms you copy and fill in — like a **Problem Scoping Canvas**, a **Research Plan**, or an **Insight Card**. They save you from starting with a blank page every time.

Playbooks are step-by-step guides for how we do recurring things — like **"How to Run a Discovery Sprint"** or **"How to Write a Trial Protocol."**

**How to use a template:** Don't edit the original! Right-click → "Make a copy", then move the copy into your project folder and fill it in there. The original stays clean for the next person.

### 📁 04 - Fleeting Notes

This is the "just dump it here" zone. Got a quick thought after a meeting? A half-formed idea? A link someone shared that you want to look at later? Drop it here.

Fleeting notes are meant to be **messy and temporary**. The only rule is: every Friday (or during our weekly vault gardening), someone goes through this folder and either moves notes to the right place, turns them into proper notes, or deletes them.

**Think of it like a kitchen counter.** It's fine to leave things on it during the day, but you tidy it up before the weekend.

### 📁 05 - Archive

Finished projects, outdated notes, things we don't need day-to-day but might want to find someday. Move stuff here instead of deleting it. Out of sight, but still searchable.

## 🔗 How to Use Links (This Is the Superpower)

Obsidian's magic trick is **linking notes together**. When you're writing a note and you mention something that has its own note in the vault, link to it by typing `[[` and then start typing the note's name.

**Example:** You're writing up a project insight and you notice participants showed status quo bias. You'd write:

"Participants consistently preferred the default option, consistent with [[Status Quo Bias\|Status Quo Bias]] — see also [[02-Knowledge Library/Behavioral Science Frameworks/Nudge Frameworks\|Nudge Frameworks]] for possible interventions."

Now those notes are connected. Over time, this web of links becomes incredibly powerful — you can see how ideas connect across projects, which methods come up most often, and which insights keep recurring.

**You can also use tags** to label notes across folders. Just add a hashtag anywhere in a note:

- #phase/scoping #phase/research #phase/design #phase/testing #phase/evaluation
- #method/interview #method/survey #method/RCT #method/observation
- #insight #decision #lesson-learned

## ✅ Our Team Agreements

These are the simple habits that keep this vault alive and useful:

**1. Capture over perfection.** A messy note in the vault beats a perfect thought stuck in your head. Write it down, even if it's rough. You (or someone else) can tidy it up later.

**2. Feed the vault after every milestone.** Finished a round of interviews? Completed a trial? Delivered a report? Spend 30 minutes writing up what happened and what you learned. Drop it in the project folder or in Fleeting Notes if you're short on time.

**3. Weekly vault gardening (10 minutes every Friday).** As a team, we spend 10 minutes at the end of our weekly meeting tidying up: moving fleeting notes to the right place, updating project statuses, flagging notes that need expanding.

**4. Don't edit someone else's note without a heads-up.** Since we share this vault through a cloud folder, ping the team chat before editing a note someone else might be working on. If you want to add your own thoughts to someone else's note, add them under a heading with your name and date.

**5. Use templates — don't reinvent the wheel.** Before starting a new document from scratch, check the Templates folder. There might already be a template for what you need.

## ❓ FAQ

**Q: Where do I put this note?** Ask yourself: "Is this about a specific project?" If yes → 01 - Projects under that project. If no → "Is this general knowledge the team can reuse?" If yes → 02 - Knowledge Library. If you're not sure → 04 - Fleeting Notes. We'll sort it out on Friday.

**Q: What if I mess something up?** Don't worry. If you're using a cloud sync tool (OneDrive, Dropbox, Google Drive), there's version history. Nothing is permanently lost. And the Archive folder is there for a reason — move, don't delete.

**Q: Do I need to write long, polished notes?** No. A three-line note with a clear title is better than no note at all. Some of our most useful notes are just a few bullet points.

**Q: Can I restructure folders or add new ones?** Bring it up at our weekly vault gardening. We evolve the structure together so it reflects how we actually work.
