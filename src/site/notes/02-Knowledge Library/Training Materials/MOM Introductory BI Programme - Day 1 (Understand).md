## 🧠 What Is This About? (1-2 sentences)

Day 1 of a two-day BI training programme for MOM staff. It covers the foundations of behavioural science (System 1 & 2 thinking, biases, nudges) and then walks participants through the "Understand" phase of the behavioural design process — Define your challenge, Discover the user journey, and Diagnose the behavioural barriers and enablers.

---

## 📌 Key Takeaways

- **Dual process theory (System 1 & 2):** People have a fast, automatic, intuitive system (System 1) and a slow, deliberate, analytical system (System 2). Most everyday behaviour is driven by System 1, which makes us susceptible to biases and heuristics.
- **Key biases covered:** Loss aversion, anchoring bias, representative heuristic, availability heuristic, choice overload, social proof, status quo/defaults, framing effects.
- **Nudges vs mandates:** Nudges preserve choice while guiding people toward better outcomes. They work by changing the choice environment rather than restricting options. But nudges have limitations — they should always be tested, and they're not a substitute for structural/policy change.
- **The 5D Behavioural Design Process:** Define → Discover → Diagnose → Design → Test. Day 1 covers the first three ("Understand" phase).
- **DEFINE:** Identify the target behaviour, target population, and why the problem matters. Write a clear problem statement with measurable outcomes.
- **DISCOVER:** Map the user's journey from awareness to action to repeated behaviour. Identify all steps, touchpoints, and decision moments. Look for friction points and dark patterns.
- **DIAGNOSE:** Use frameworks like [[02-Knowledge Library/Behavioral Science Frameworks/COM-B Model - Michie et al\|COM-B]] to identify the behavioural fuels (enablers) and frictions (barriers) at each step of the journey. Ask: Is this a capability, opportunity, or motivation problem?
- **User research primer:** Covers secondary data (admin data, clickstream, app usage) and primary methods (in-depth interviews, focus group discussions, observation, surveys). Includes tips on projection/personification techniques in interviews.

---

## 💡 Insights Worth Remembering

- The training uses hands-on cluster exercises where MOM teams work on real policy challenges (e.g., encouraging voluntary uptake, migrant worker behaviours, business leader engagement) — this is a good model for how we might run our own workshops.
- The "journey map" exercise is practical: map every step a user takes, then ask "how many steps does it take to reach the desired outcome?" and "what can we remove to make things more seamless?"
- COM-B is used as the primary diagnostic framework — each barrier/enabler gets mapped to Capability (psychological/physical), Opportunity (social/physical), or Motivation (reflective/automatic).
- The training acknowledges that some earlier BI findings (like "signing at the top") have failed replication — it takes the replication crisis seriously and emphasises always testing.
- Holistic frameworks like COM-B are recommended over simple nudge-based approaches, because they force you to understand behaviour before jumping to solutions.
