## 🧠 What Is This About? (1-2 sentences)

The COM-B model says that any behaviour requires three things: **Capability** (can I do it?), **Opportunity** (does my environment allow it?), and **Motivation** (do I want to do it more than other things?). To change a behaviour, you need to shift one or more of these three components.

---

## 📌 Key Takeaways

- **Behaviour = Capability + Opportunity + Motivation.** All three must be present for a behaviour to happen. If any one is missing, the behaviour won't occur.
- **Capability** splits into psychological (knowledge, skills, mental stamina) and physical (strength, physical skill, stamina).
- **Opportunity** splits into physical (time, location, resources, environment) and social (cultural norms, social cues, what people around you do).
- **Motivation** splits into reflective (conscious plans, evaluations, decisions) and automatic (habits, impulses, desires, emotional responses).
- The components interact with each other AND with behaviour itself — so changing behaviour also changes its determinants, creating a feedback loop that supports long-term change.

---

## 💡 Insights Worth Remembering

- This is an **interactional model**, not a one-way street. When someone starts exercising (behaviour change), they build skills (capability goes up), it becomes routine (opportunity normalises), and they feel good about it (motivation increases). The change feeds itself.
- Capability and opportunity both feed into motivation — so even if someone isn't motivated yet, giving them skills and removing barriers can spark motivation on its own.
- A key diagnostic question for any project: "Is the target audience not doing this because they CAN'T (capability), because nothing around them supports it (opportunity), or because they don't WANT to enough (motivation)?" The answer tells you where to intervene.
- COM-B sits at the centre of the larger [[02-Knowledge Library/Behavioral Science Frameworks/Behaviour Change Wheel - Michie et al\|Behaviour Change Wheel - Michie et al]], which adds nine intervention functions and seven policy categories around it.
